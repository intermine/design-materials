Research Interest

My group uses computational and experimental methods to investigate basic biological processes. We also build databases to exploit the large volumes of data being generated by modern biology. I am involved in cancer biology through collaboration with Bertie Gottgens of the CIMR.

- integration and analysis of diverse large-scale genomics datasets
- modelling of developmental processes
- small RNA function
- characterising transcriptional control networks
- synthetic biology
- computational methods
- databases
- gene regulation
- genomics
- bioinformatics

Background

With a background in molecular biology and a long-term interest in computing my lab concentrates both on bioinformatics and related wet-lab work. Before joining the Genetics Department I spent several years running bioinformatics in a start-up biotechnology company and before that three years at the nascent Wellcome Trust Sanger Institute. Since 2004 I also have been the Director of the Cambridge Computational Biology Institute, based in the Department of Applied Mathematics and Theoretical Physics.
